De Moivre's Theorem

De Moivre's Theorem states that for any real number \(x\) and integer \(n\):

\({\displaystyle {\big (}\cos x+i\sin x{\big )}^{n}=\cos nx+i\sin nx}\)

Euler's Formula

Euler's Formula states that for any real number \(x\):

\({\displaystyle e^{ix}=\cos x+i\sin x}\)

Application

De Moivre's Theorem and Euler's Formula can be used together to easily manipulate complex numbers. For example, we can easily evaluate something like \((1+i)^8\) by writing it as:

\((\sqrt{2}(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}+\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}i))^8=16(\cos \frac{\pi}{4}+i\sin \frac{\pi}{4})^8=16(\cos 2\pi+i\sin 2\pi)=16.\)

Also, we can find \(i^i\) by writing:

\(i^i=(e^{i(\frac{\pi}{2}+2\pi k)})^i=e^{-\frac{\pi}{2}-2\pi k},\)

in which \(k\) is any integer.
